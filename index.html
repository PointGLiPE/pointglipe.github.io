Overview

Point clouds are inherently irregular, and their positional information contains rich semantic cues. To effectively perform positional encoding for point clouds, it is crucial to account for their intrinsic characteristics. In this paper, we revisit the fundamental role of positional encoding in point cloud learning and propose PointGLiPE, a global‚Äìlocal integrated positional encoding framework that explicitly models multi-scale global references, local geometric structures, and context-aware neighborhood relations. PointGLiPE consists of three modules: Multi-Scale Positional Encoding (MSPE) for capturing cross-level spatial context, Local Geometric Positional Encoding (LGPE) for fine-grained shape representation, and Local Relational Positional Encoding (LRPE) for adaptive neighborhood relation modeling. The framework can be seamlessly integrated into U‚ÄìNetstyle point cloud backbones to achieve effective positional encoding for point clouds. Extensive experiments on five public datasets for classification and segmentation demonstrate the generality and effectiveness of PointGLiPE. Ablation studies and Shapley-value analyses further verify the complementary contributions of the three modules.

Motivation

Illustration of positional encoding differences between images and point clouds. From a global perspective (left), an image can be viewed as a two-dimensional projection of a threedimensional scene, where adjacent pixels in the 2D plane may correspond to spatially distant regions in 3D space. In contrast, the distances between points in a point cloud faithfully preserve the true geometric relationships, providing an intrinsic spatial organization that directly reflects the underlying scene geometry. Moreover, image coordinates are defined on a regular and topologically connected grid, which inherently encodes a consistent notion of spatial order. Point clouds, however, are irregular, unordered, and permutation-invariant‚Äîeach point independently resides in continuous 3D space, and its absolute coordinate alone offers no explicit clue about its structural role or semantic position within an object or environment. From a local perspective (right), positional encodings in images mainly serve as spatial indices, helping recover the original 2D arrangement that may be lost during tokenization or flattening operations in transformers. Such encodings primarily convey location identity but lack deeper geometric or semantic information. In contrast, the coordinates of points inherently embed rich semantic and geometric cues, reflecting object shape, part composition, and spatial hierarchy. Furthermore, when encoding relative positional relationships among points, it is essential to account for the local geometric context‚Äîincluding neighborhood shape, point density, and scale variation‚Äîsince these factors significantly influence the interpretation of spatial relations. Consequently, naively relying on coordinate differences or Euclidean distances often fails to capture fine-grained local geometry, motivating the design of context-aware positional encoding mechanisms tailored for irregular 3D point sets.

Overview of the proposed framework. The network adopts a U-Net‚Äìstyle architecture for point cloud semantic segmentation and classification, consisting of an encoder‚Äìdecoder structure. The positional encoding mechanism is integrated into the encoder and comprises three complementary modules: Multi-Scale Positional Encoding (MSPE) for global spatial encoding, Local Geometric Positional Encoding (LGPE) and Local Relational Positional Encoding (LRPE) for fine-grained local context modeling.

Conclusion

We propose PointGLiPE, a positional encoding framework tailored to the unique characteristics of point clouds for 3D understanding. Unlike existing methods that rely solely on single-scale or purely geometric cues, PointGLiPE integrates three complementary encoding branches: (1) Multi-Scale Positional Encoding (MSPE) to capture global spatial context, (2) Local Geometric Positional Encoding (LGPE) to extract fine-grained structural cues, and (3) Local Relational Positional Encoding (LRPE) to model adaptive neighborhood interactions. Together, these modules enhance global--local representation learning and relational reasoning in point-based networks. Extensive experiments on five standard benchmarks---ModelNet40, ScanObjectNN, ShapeNetPart, S3DIS, and ScanNet v2---demonstrate consistent improvements in overall accuracy and mean IoU across various tasks, validating the effectiveness and generality of the proposed design. Comprehensive ablation studies further confirm the contribution of each module within the integrated framework.
